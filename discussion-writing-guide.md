# Discussion Writing Guide

The discussion should interpret, not repeat results.

## Practical flow
- First paragraph: one sentence with the main finding, one sentence with why it matters
- Middle: compare directly against the 2-4 most relevant prior studies
- Limitations: name concrete weaknesses and likely effect direction
- Final paragraph: realistic next step, not broad promises

## Red flags
- Claiming broad clinical impact from early preclinical data
- Hiding negative or null findings in vague language
- Mixing speculation with confirmed results
